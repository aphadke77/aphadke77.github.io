🏠 Home Buyers' Plan (HBP)

Withdraw up to $60,000  ($120,000 for couples)
  • Use funds to buy or build a qualifying home
  • Must be a first-time home buyer (or not owned in past 4 years)
  • Repayment: 15 years, starting 2 years after withdrawal
  • Missed repayments are added to taxable income
  • Minimum annual repayment: withdrawal ÷ 15

Example: Withdraw $60,000 → Repay $4,000/year for 15 years

🏫 Lifelong Learning Plan (LLP)

Withdraw up to $10,000/year  (max $20,000 total)
  • Use funds for full-time education or training for you or your spouse
  • Must be enrolled in a qualifying educational program
  • Can withdraw over a 4-year period
  • Repayment: 10 years, starting 5 years after first withdrawal
  • Missed repayments are added to taxable income

Example: Withdraw $20,000 total → Repay $2,000/year for 10 years

⚠️ Regular RRSP Withdrawals (Not Penalty-Free)

  • Withholding tax: 10% (up to $5k), 20% ($5k–$15k), 30% (over $15k)
  • Full amount added to taxable income for the year
  • You permanently lose that contribution room
  • Additional tax may be owed at filing if your marginal rate exceeds withholding

Consider withdrawing in retirement when your income and tax rate may be lower.

Note: This calculator provides estimates based on consistent returns and contributions. Actual RRSP performance will vary based on market conditions and investment choices. The 2025 RRSP contribution limit is 18% of earned income (up to $32,490) minus pension adjustments. Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice.
